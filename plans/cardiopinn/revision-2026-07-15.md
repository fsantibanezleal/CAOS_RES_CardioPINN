## F1 [HIGH / content] Implementation page, 'ECGi: reconstruction' tab (Callout)
**Issue:** The ECGi 'Results' numbers are the plain-Tikhonov metrics, but the reconstruction the app actually bakes and displays is the deep-ensemble mean, whose metrics are worse; the headline overstates the accuracy of the shown field.
**Evidence:** Implementation.tsx:107 reports 'human sinus RE 0.65 / CC 0.72, PVP 0.58 / 0.80, AVP 0.54 / 0.85; dog sinus 0.54 / 0.78'. These equal catalogue.json relative_error_tikhonov/correlation_tikhonov exactly (0.654/0.718, 0.579/0.804, 0.539/0.845, 0.542/0.779). But the equation caption at Implementation.tsx:105 states 'the ensemble mean is the reconstruction', and the pipeline bakes exactly that field: ecgi_edgar.py:145 `rec = recon["ensemble"]["rec"]`. The ensemble/graph metrics baked alongside are worse: sinus 0.667/0.722, PVP 0.667/0.714, AVP 0.573/0.802, dog 0.588/0.731. So the displayed reconstruction has RE 0.67/CC 0.71 for PVP, not the reported 0.58/0.80 (a 0.09 absolute CC overstatement); the graph prior + ensemble the section is titled after actually degrade accuracy vs the number shown.
**Fix:** Either relabel the callout numbers as 'best single method (zeroth-order Tikhonov)' and add the ensemble/graph numbers that correspond to the displayed field, or report the ensemble metrics (sinus 0.67/0.72, PVP 0.67/0.71, AVP 0.57/0.80, dog 0.59/0.73) so the headline matches both the equation caption and the baked recovered_mV field.

## F2 [HIGH / content] Experiments doc / Protocol (leakage-safe) tab, SVG + prose
**Issue:** The page states the held-out heart cage is 'never seen by the inverse' and that fitting the gain on the cage is FORBIDDEN, but the pipeline fits the scalar forward gain using the held-out cage potentials, and then scores over the full beat.
**Evidence:** ecgi_edgar.py:84-85 computes `pred = A @ cage_p` then `gain = (pred[:, :nt//2] * torso_p[:, :nt//2]).sum() / (pred[:, :nt//2]**2).sum()` -> the forward gain (Ag = gain*A, used by the inverse solve) is fit against cage_p, the held-out gold-standard heart-cage potentials, on the first half of frames. This directly contradicts Experiments.tsx:33-37 SVG text ('true potentials, used ONLY to compute RE / CC / UQ', 'never seen by the inverse', struck-out 'FORBIDDEN: fit lambda or the gain on the held-out cage (leakage)'). Also Experiments.tsx:64 prose 'the forward model is never tuned on the evaluation window' is inaccurate: evaluate() scores over the FULL beat (ecgi_edgar.py:121 `_re_cc(rec, cage_p)`, :131 `n_time_frames = cage_p.shape[1]` = 244/236/220/593), which includes the first-half calibration window. Because the gain sets overall magnitude and RE is a norm-ratio metric, the reported RE is partly informed by the truth.
**Fix:** State honestly that the single scalar gain is fit against the first-half heart-cage potentials (a temporal split that uses the truth, not a body-only calibration), disclosed the same way the oracle-lambda callout is. Remove the SVG's 'cage used ONLY to score / never seen by the inverse' and the struck-out 'fit the gain on the cage is FORBIDDEN' framing, OR change evaluate() to score only the held-out second half so 'never tuned on the evaluation window' becomes true.

## F6 [HIGH / robustness] data-pipeline/cardiopinnlab/real/flow4d_bake.py + flow4d_dicom.py
**Issue:** Coords length-unit inconsistency with no guard: the loader returns coords in mm but the bake treats them as meters, and the PPE solve uses h in meters; the engine is provably 1000x-sensitive to this.
**Evidence:** flow4d_dicom.py:62 docstring 'coords [Nvox,3] mm' and _voxel_xyz (lines 52-58) build coords straight from PixelSpacing/ImagePositionPatient (DICOM mm; probed real file: PixelSpacing 2.5\2.5, ImagePositionPatient -140.0..168.3) with NO mm->m conversion (grep for 1000/1e-3/0.001 in the loader: none). flow4d_bake.py:41 labels the same array '# m', passes it as coords_m to denoise_frame/train_spacetime (flow4d_denoise.py:69-70 documents '(m)'), and at line 126 multiplies by 1000 'mm' (author believes it is meters). The PINN scales analytic derivatives by U/L with L computed from these values, then solve_ppe_precomputed uses h=2.5e-3 (flow4d_bake.py:42, meters). I confirmed with a numpy PPE test on the analytic converging duct that computing source/flux with per-mm vs per-meter derivatives while solving with h in meters changes the recovered pressure drop by exactly 1000x (4.74 -> 0.0047 mmHg). The shipped trace (0.79 mmHg) is order-consistent with Bernoulli (2.51), so I cannot confirm end-to-end corruption without a torch run (UNVERIFIED on the shipped number), but there is a real unguarded 1000x unit mismatch. Compounding: h is hardcoded, not derived from the DICOM PixelSpacing/SliceThickness (both 2.5mm here), so a scan at a different spacing silently gets the wrong pressure scale, and the check_artifacts gate 0<drop<60 (check_artifacts.py:86) is far too loose to catch a scale error (0.0008 mmHg would pass).
**Fix:** Assert coords units at ingest (e.g. lumen extent in [0.01,0.5] m) and convert mm->m explicitly in load_4dflow; derive h from PixelSpacing/SpacingBetweenSlices instead of hardcoding 2.5e-3; tighten the gate to require the PPE drop to bracket Bernoulli within a physiological band rather than 0<drop<60.
